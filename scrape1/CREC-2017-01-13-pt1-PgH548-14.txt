            By Mr. SERRANO:
        H.R. 571.
        Congress has the power to enact this legislation pursuant 
     to the following:
       Clause 2 of section 5 ofarticle I of the Constitution, 
     which states: ``Each House may determine the Rules of its 
     Proceedings, punish its Members fordisorderly Behaviour, and, 
     with the Concurrence of two thirds, expel a Member.''
       Additionally, Congress has the power toenact this 
     legislation under Clause 2 of section 3 of article IV of the 
     Constitution, which states that ``The Congress shallhave 
     Power to dispose of and make all needful Rules and 
     Regulations respecting the Territory or other Property 
     belonging to theUnited States; and nothing in this 
     Constitution shall be so construed as to Prejudice any Claims 
     of the United States, or of anyparticular State.''

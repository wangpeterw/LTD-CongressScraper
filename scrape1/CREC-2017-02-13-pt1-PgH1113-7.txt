  HONORING INVESTMENTS IN RECRUITING AND EMPLOYING AMERICAN MILITARY 
                          VETERANS ACT OF 2017

  The SPEAKER pro tempore. The unfinished business is the vote on the 
motion to suspend the rules and pass the bill (H.R. 244) to encourage 
effective, voluntary private sector investments to recruit, employ, and 
retain men and women who have served in the United States military with 
annual presidential awards to private sector employers recognizing such 
efforts, and for other purposes, as amended, on which the yeas and nays 
were ordered.
  The Clerk read the title of the bill.
  The SPEAKER pro tempore. The question is on the motion offered by the 
gentleman from Florida (Mr. Bilirakis) that the House suspend the rules 
and pass the bill, as amended.
  The vote was taken by electronic device, and there were--yeas 409, 
nays 1, not voting 21, as follows:

  Mrs. DEMINGS, Mr. BUCK, Ms. ROYBAL-ALLARD, and Mr. HUFFMAN changed 
their vote from ``nay'' to ``yea.''
  So (two-thirds being in the affirmative) the rules were suspended and 
the bill, as amended, was passed.
  The result of the vote was announced as above recorded.
  The title of the bill was amended so as to read: ``A bill to 
encourage effective, voluntary investments to recruit, employ, and 
retain men and women who have served in the United States military with 
annual Federal awards to employers recognizing such efforts, and for 
other purposes.''.
  A motion to reconsider was laid on the table.

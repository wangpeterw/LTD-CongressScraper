                            MORNING BUSINESS

  The PRESIDING OFFICER. Under the previous order, the Senate will be 
in a period of morning business, with Senators permitted to speak for 
up to 10 minutes each.
  (At the request of Mr. Schumer, the following statement was ordered 
to be printed in the Record.)
 Mr. DURBIN. Mr. President, I was necessarily absent for the 
votes on the nomination of Jay Clayton to be a Member of the Securities 
and Exchange Commission, Executive Calendar No. 36.
  On vote No. 118, had I been present, I would have voted nay on the 
confirmation of the Clayton nomination, Executive Calendar No. 
36.

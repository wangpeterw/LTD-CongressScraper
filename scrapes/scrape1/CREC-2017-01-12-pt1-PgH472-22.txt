            By Mr. BURGESS:
       H.R. 487.
        Congress has the power to enact this legislation pursuant 
     to the following:
       Under Article I, Section VIII,Clause 1, ``The Congress 
     shall have power to lay and collect taxes, duties, imposts 
     and excises, to pay the debts and providefor the common 
     defense and general welfare of the United States . . .'' In 
     addition, Article I, Section VIII, Clause 14provides, ``To 
     make rules for the government and regulation of the land and 
     naval forces.'' Lastly, Article I, Section VIII,Clause 16 
     states ``The Congress shall have Power To provide for 
     organizing, arming, and disciplining, the Militia, and 
     forgoverning such Part of them as may be employed in the 
     Service of the United States, reserving to the States 
     respectively, theAppointment of the Officers, and the 
     Authority of training the Militia according to the discipline 
     prescribed byCongress.''

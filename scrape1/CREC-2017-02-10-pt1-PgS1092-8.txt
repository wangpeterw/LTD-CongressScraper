                 PERMITTING USE OF THE CAPITOL ROTUNDA

  Mr. BOOZMAN. Madam President, I ask unanimous consent that the Chair 
lay before the Senate H. Con. Res. 18, which was received from the 
House.
  The PRESIDING OFFICER. The clerk will report the concurrent 
resolution by title.
  The bill clerk read as follows:

       A concurrent resolution (H. Con. Res. 18) permitting the 
     use of the rotunda of the Capitol for a ceremony as part of 
     the commemoration of the days of remembrance of victims of 
     the Holocaust.

  There being no objection, the Senate proceeded to consider the 
concurrent resolution.

[[Page S1093]]

  

  Mr. BOOZMAN. I ask unanimous consent that the concurrent resolution 
be agreed to and the motion to reconsider be considered made and laid 
upon the table with no intervening action or debate.
  The PRESIDING OFFICER. Without objection, it is so ordered.
  The concurrent resolution (H. Con. Res. 18) was agreed to.

What's Our Mission?

Congress appears to be a "black-box" of sorts nowadays. With the especially heated political climate throughout the past few years, it's critical that there be transparency between the work thats being done in Congress to the average citizen.

There are public, government-provided online records of almost every word spoken by representatives, witnesses, and senators on a daily basis. However, to make use of this information, one has navigate through many pages, find a specific days' discourse to view, and then sift through thousands of lines of a single .txt file to find any information of use.

To the average citizen and especially researchers interested in this dataset, this is far too time-consuming and inaccessible to yield any meaningful results or analysis of what's actually going on behind the scenes in Congress.

That's where our team comes in with Searchlight, a liberating textual data project, sponsored by Goodly Labs. Our mission was to create an accessible database of information for citizens and researchers alike, where one can simply query for speeches within Congress, sifting through our scraped and parsed congressional data to find a set of speech snippets that can be downloaded into a csv.

Search for speeches by a given represenative or senator, or maybe by party. Maybe particular month or date is of interest to you, or maybe speeches given by delegates from a certain state. Whatever it be, our project helps you to collect information that best matches your needs.

If you liked this project, please give us a like on Facebook! We hope to continue to partner with Goodly Labs and the UC Berkeley Institute Data Science to bring projects like this to the public sector!
